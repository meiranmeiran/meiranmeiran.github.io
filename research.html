Overarching Goal

Microbiomes, communities of bacteria, archaea, viruses, and tiny eukaryotes, are closely related to human well-being, sustainable development, and planery health. They also possess extraordinarily diversity that can be harnessed for safe and sustainable water provision and treatment. Despite recent advances, vast metabolic potential remains unexplored. Our group combines genomics, evolution, and engineering design to decode complex microbiomes in engineered and natural water environments and devise microbiome-informed solutions for sustainable water systems.

Research Theme 1. Microbial Evolution in Engineered Ecosystems

In the last centry, the synthesis of over 80,000 new chemicals has transformed human life but also brought significant health and environmental risks. Many of these man-made pollutants are completely new to the microbes we employ in wastewater treatment, challenging existing processes. However, wastewater treatment environments may also drive microbial evolution, fostering new metabolic capabilities to break down anthropogenic pollutants. We aspire to leverage advanced evolutionary analyses and well-controlled microbiology experiments, to discover new microbial genes and species that could be used to develop new remediation strategies.
