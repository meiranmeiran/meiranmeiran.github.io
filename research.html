Overarching Goal

Microbiomes, communities of bacteria, archaea, viruses, and tiny eukaryotes, are closely related to human well-being, sustainable development, and planery health. They also possess extraordinarily diversity that can be harnessed for safe and sustainable water provision and treatment. Despite recent advances, vast metabolic potential remains unexplored. Our group combines genomics, evolution, and engineering design to decode complex microbiomes in engineered and natural water environments and devise microbiome-informed solutions for sustainable water systems.

Research Theme 1. Microbial Evolution in Engineered Ecosystems

In the last centry, the synthesis of over 80,000 new chemicals has transformed human life but also brought significant health and environmental risks. Many of these man-made pollutants are completely new to the microbes we employ in wastewater treatment, challenging existing processes. However, wastewater treatment environments may also drive microbial evolution, fostering new metabolic capabilities to break down anthropogenic pollutants. We aspire to discover new microbial genes and species that could be used to develop new remediation strategies.

Research Theme 2. Microbial Biomass Degraders for Waste Decomposition

Microbial biomass, including the cells themselves, constitutes a significant portion of the Earth's organic carbon pool, especially in oxygen-poor environments like water sediments and wastewater treatment systems. Unraveling the mechanisms of efficient biomass degradation is key to understanding the global carbon cycle and enhancing waste decomposition. Our goal is to uncover the vast and largely untapped diversity of microbial cell degraders. Eventually, we aim to identify microbial probiotics that can optimize both centralized water treatment processes and sanitation in resource-constrained communities.

Research Theme 3. Bridging Biology and Physics for Efficient Bioreactors

The interdependence between biological processes and physical dynamics is vital for bioreactor efficiency. But it is often overlooked when we analyze the microbiome in bioreactors. We advocate for a comprehensive investigation of the intricate biology-physics interplay by coupled quantification of the microbial and physical dynamics. By working with municipal facilities and industries, we aim to provide microbiome-informed bioreactor design and operation towards reduced footprints and enhanced efficiencies.
