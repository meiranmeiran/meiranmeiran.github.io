Ran Mei
Ran Mei

I am now a JSPS postdoc #OPENTOWORK# working with Dr. Masaru K. NOBU and Dr. Hiroyuki Imachi at the Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology (JAMSTEC) in Japan. I work in both natural and engineered ecosystems to understand fundamental microbial processes and seek for solutions that promote a sustainable microbiology-driven waste-resource-health nexus. I hold a Ph.D. (2020) and an M.S. (2015), both in Environmental Science and Engineering, from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), and a B.S. in Energy and Resource Engineering (2013) from Peking University, China.

Collaborators

Yoichi Kamagata
Yoichi Kamagata

Dr. Kamagata is the Assistant Director General of the Department of Life Science and Biotechnology at the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST). His research interests include diversity of microbes in natural environment, syntrophy between organisms in anoxic/oxic environment, degradation of anthropogenic chemicals by organisms, molecular physiology and domestication of uncultured organisms, detection of target genes/organisms, and degradation of natural polymers.

Takashi Narihiro
Takashi Narihiro

Dr. Narihiro is the Group Leader of the Microbial Ecology and Technology Research Group at the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST) in Japan. His research interests include deciphering the ecophysiology of microbes in wastewater treatment systems; ecological and environmental engineering research employing microbial community analysis; exploration of novel microbial functions based on (meta)genomic information.
